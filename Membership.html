How to Become a Member of Semco

Qualification for Membership

Membership in this Cooperative is open to all natural persons who are Filipino citizens of legal age, with the capacity to enter into contracts, and who fall within the membership classification defined as RESIDENTIAL.

Regular Member:

  • Regular employee of Semirara Mining and Power Corporation (SMPC) and its subsidiaries, namely: DMC-Construction Equipment Resources, Inc. (DMC-CERI), Divine Word School of Semirara Island, Inc. (DWSSII), and Semirara Training Center, Inc. (STCI), residing within the area of operation as defined in Article VI of the Articles of Cooperation.
  • Regular Employee of Semirara Multi-Purpose Cooperative (SEMCO).
  • Former employees of SEMCO who continue their membership and reside on Semirara Island.
  • Probationary or contractual employees of SEMCO.
  • SMPC sub-contractors, employees of SMPC suppliers, or Public/Private School Teachers residing within the defined area as per Article VI of the Articles of Cooperation.
  • Regular members of SEMCO who had previously terminated their membership yet continue to reside and work on Semirara Island.
  • Non-Teaching Staff of a Public or Private School; Operators/Members of SEMTODA only; Market Vendors; or Business Owners residing within the defined area of operation.

Associate members meeting the minimum requirements for regular membership, who continuously patronize the Cooperative for two (2) years and express their intention to remain members, shall be upgraded to regular membership.

Requirements for Membership:

A prospective member must comply with the following requirements:

  • Submit an approved application for membership.
  • Provide a Certificate of Completion for the prescribed Pre-Membership Education Seminar (PMES).
  • Subscribe and pay the required minimum share capital along with the membership fee.
  • Actively patronize the services of the Cooperative.

Application for Membership:

Applicants shall submit a duly completed membership form to the Board of Directors, who will render a decision within seven (7) days from receipt. The form collects the applicantâ€™s personal information and outlines their obligation to participate in programs related to capital build-up and savings mobilization. It also contains an undertaking to abide by the by-laws, policies, guidelines, rules, and regulations set by both the Board of Directors and the General Assembly. Note: A membership fee of One Hundred Pesos (Php100.00) must accompany the application, and this fee shall be refunded if the application is rejected.

Appeal:

If an application is denied by the Board of Directors, the applicant may appeal to the General Assembly by notifying the Secretary of the Cooperative within thirty (30) days prior to the next General Assembly meeting. The decision of the General Assembly shall be final.

Minimum Share Capital Requirements:

An applicant must subscribe to a minimum of one hundred fifty (150) shares and pay the corresponding amount for at least five (5) shares upon approval of membership.
